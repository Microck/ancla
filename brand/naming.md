# Ancla Naming

## Chosen name

- `Ancla`

## Why it fits

- `Ancla` means `anchor` in Spanish, which matches the core product mechanic.
- The name implies a fixed physical point outside the phone, which is exactly how release works.
- It is short, distinct, and reads cleanly in UI, App Store metadata, and icon labels.
- It supports the slate-and-anchor visual system without forcing nautical kitsch.

## Product meaning

For this product, an `ancla` is the paired physical NFC object that keeps the release path out in the room instead of on the same glass surface as the distraction.

## Pronunciation

- Spanish: `AHN-clah`
- English-friendly reading: `AHN-klah`

## Suggested supporting copy

- Short descriptor: `Physical friction for iPhone app blocking`
- App Store subtitle direction: `Block apps until you scan the anchor`
- One-line product explanation: `Ancla pairs Screen Time shielding with one NFC sticker so unlocking distracting apps takes movement and intent.`

## Naming guardrails

- Use `Ancla` as the product name, not `ANCLA`, except for occasional display typography inside the app UI.
- Keep `anchor` as the explanatory English metaphor, not the primary brand name.
- Avoid adding extra nautical language like `harbor`, `captain`, `crew`, or `voyage`.
- Keep the language minimal, physical, and behavioral.
